⚖️ Verdicts — one combined judgement per food
ℹ️ How to read this tab

What this is: one plain verdict per food, so you don't have to cross-read the other analysis tabs. It answers a single question — does this food react more, less, or about the same as your typical food?

Why “compared to your typical food”: your body reacts to most things — your baseline ✕ rate is high (shown at the top of the list). So “totally safe” isn't realistic here; instead every food is graded against your own average. A food that reacts less than usual is a genuine win.

The four groups:

  • 🔺 Suspect — reacts MORE than your average. Your prime candidates to cut or test.
  • 🟡 Typical — behaves like your average food. Neutral.
  • 🟢 Easier on you — reacts LESS than your average. Your safer choices — lean on these during flares.
  • ❔ Unclear — you haven't marked enough reaction windows for this food yet.

The small tags: strong / some / weak evidence = how many reaction windows back the verdict (more windows = more trustworthy). 📌 staple = you eat it almost daily, so no day-comparison can isolate it — use 🧪 Test to know for sure.

How to act: tap 🧪 Test on any Suspect to run a controlled trial that actually proves whether it's the culprit.
